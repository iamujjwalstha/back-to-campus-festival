Festival Zones

More Than
Just Food

Two dedicated zones make sure every student — whether social or shy, energetic or tired — has a space where they feel comfortable and at home.

🌿
Green Zone
Eco-Friendly by Design
Every choice made for this festival — from cups to waste sorting — is guided by a commitment to the environment. Students don't just attend a sustainable event; they learn from one.
♻️ Colour-coded waste sorting stations throughout the grounds
🥤 Reusable cups and compostable packaging at all food stalls
🌱 No single-use plastics — eco practices are visible and explained
📋 Info boards showing the festival's environmental footprint in real time
🧘
Chill Zone
Your Quiet Corner
Unlike the forced awkward games of the past, this zone is built around real connection. A low-pressure space for students who need a moment to breathe — or simply want to make a friend without the noise.
🪑 Comfortable seating away from the main stage area
🎨 Relaxed creative activities — drawing, postcards, shared boards
💬 Conversation starter cards for students who find small talk hard
🤝 JAMKO peer supporters present — friendly, not formal
📱 Mental health info & campus support contacts displayed clearly

Good to Know

Practical Info

Everything a new student needs to know before showing up. No surprises.

📅 Sept 1–2, 2026 · From 12:00
📍 Rajakatu 35, Jyväskylä
🎟 Free entry — no ticket needed
🚫 Alcohol-free event
Do I need to register or buy a ticket?
No registration, no ticket. Just show up at the JAMK main campus courtyard on the day. Entry is completely free for all students — new, returning, and international. The only thing you'll receive at the door is your wristband.
Is alcohol allowed at the festival?
No alcohol is served or permitted at the Back to Campus Festival. This is an intentional choice — the event is designed so that every single student, regardless of age, background, or personal values, can participate fully and comfortably.
How does the wristband and food discount work?
You receive a wristband with a unique barcode at the entrance. Show it at any food stall and your very first purchase is 50% off. After that one scan, the barcode is marked as used — all further orders are full price. Your repeat purchases directly support that stall in the competition.
What if I don't know anyone and feel nervous?
That feeling is completely normal — and it's exactly why we built the Chill Zone. It's a quiet area away from the stage with comfortable seating, low-pressure activities, and JAMKO peer supporters nearby. You can ease into the day at your own pace. Almost every new student arrives feeling the same way.
What happens if the weather is bad?
The festival is planned as an outdoor event, but a weather contingency plan is in place. If conditions are unsuitable, sections of the festival move to covered areas on campus. Check JAMK's official social media and campus boards on the day for any updates.
Can international students participate fully?
Yes — the festival is designed with international students at its core. The food stalls, music, workshops, and volunteer welcomers all reflect a multicultural community. The key message is simple: You Belong Here. Come As You Are.
Where exactly is the festival located?
The festival takes place at the JAMK main campus courtyard, Rajakatu 35, 40200 Jyväskylä. The campus is easy to reach by bus or on foot from the city centre. Volunteer welcomers will be visible at the entrance to guide you in.
